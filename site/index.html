From playlist to finished mix

Four steps. Most of them run on their own.

1

Import

Paste a YouTube playlist, pick the tracks you want, or drag and drop your own video files straight onto the track list.

2

Detect

Every track is analyzed automatically: BPM, key, and drops found by sub-bass energy, so vocal choruses never fool it.

3

Arrange

Add drops to the timeline, trim on a zoomable waveform with a beat grid, and hear the real crossfades in the live preview.

4

Render

One click renders a branded 1080p mix with titles and intro, plus a vertical Short of the best drop for YouTube Shorts.

The hard parts, handled

Everything a drop mix needs to sound intentional instead of stitched together.

🎶

Real drop detection

Candidates are scored on low-band energy under 150 Hz and validated against bass lift and kick periodicity.

🪖

Kick-aligned seams

Every crossfade measures the actual kick grid on both sides and nudges itself so the incoming kick lands on the downbeat.

🎧

Live preview

Equal-power crossfades in the browser with the video following along, so what you hear is what renders.

🎬

Branded output

Full-frame channel overlay, track titles in your own fonts, and an intro animation timed to end exactly on the first kick.

📱

Shorts, included

Each render can also produce a vertical Short cut around the strongest drop, ready to upload.

📁

Projects

Each mix lives in its own workspace with its own tracks and exports. Switch projects without mixing up libraries.

🔊

Consistent loudness

Every clip is normalized to -14 LUFS, the YouTube target, and previews are matched so there are no surprises.

🔒

Local first

Everything runs on your machine. Your library, analysis and renders never leave your computer.
